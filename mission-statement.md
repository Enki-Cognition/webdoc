# Mission Statement

The Enki Cognition Project exists to improve the reliability of knowledge generation by developing transparent, human-centered research methodologies and standards grounded in epistemic humility and rigorous validation.

“Developing transparent, human-centered research standards to improve knowledge generation.”

## Commitment

Because we prioritize knowledge over dogma,we are  committed to discarding any belief that is contradicted by any valid data points. This process can make people feel very uncomfortable. If you feel an immediate urge to argue, dismiss, or rationalize away a data point we present, please recognize that this is a standard biological instinct when our previous experiences are challenged. If we work through that discomfort together we can get to a comfortable solution.

We are not interested in "winning" an argument (or debating or negotiating); that causes hidden biases within the reasoning process. We are interested in understanding problems and developing trustworthy solutions. Any false beliefs undermine this process. If you have a data point that contradicts my model, we want it and require it. We will not be offended; instead we will be grateful, because it allows us to improve our understanding.

We ask for the same courtesy: let us look at the data points together and strive to understand, rather than defending our previous conclusions.

## Knowledge traceability 

Reasoning requires knowledge and knowledge require an understanding of why and how well you know something. Knowledge requires a foundation of of knowledge, otherwise it is built on a foundation of intuition (what feels right) rather than valid justification. 

To address this challenge this project aims to create a set of knowledge and reasoning artifacts for understanding the traceability to fundamental concepts.
