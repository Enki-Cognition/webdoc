# Why reason in everyday life?

We all naturally rely on our instincts and emotions to interpret the world and make decisions, which sometimes leads us to feel confident even when our conclusions aren’t fully aligned with reality. This process—often called rationalization—is a normal, useful habit that helps us maintain comfort and coherence. Reasoning, by contrast, is a tool that complements our instincts: it introduces observation, testing, and feedback to check whether our conclusions actually work in the real world. Using reasoning doesn’t diminish how you feel; it simply provides a clearer view of reality, helping your confidence match what truly works. Think of it as a safety check for your intuition rather than a critique of it.

Today, the world is changing rapidly and our past experiences aren’t working as well.  transitioning to a more reasoned approach will help address this challenge.  

“We all want to make decisions that work well in the real world and avoid unnecessary errors.”

“Sometimes our brains shortcut reality to feel comfortable—this is natural and can help in the short term.”

“Reasoning is just a way to check whether our conclusions actually work in the real world. It doesn’t stop you from feeling good; it helps you feel confident that your feelings match reality.”

“You might notice situations where your intuition feels right, but checking with reasoning gives a better chance of success. It’s like a safety check, not a criticism.”
